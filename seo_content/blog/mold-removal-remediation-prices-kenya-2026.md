## Why Professional Mold Remediation isn't Just "Cleaning"

It's common to mistake mold remediation for deep cleaning. If it were just dirt, wiping it away would suffice. However, mold is a living fungus. Its roots penetrate porous materials (like drywall, wood, and concrete), and agitated spores can contaminate the entire house through the air.

Professional remediation involves:

1. **Source Identification:** Finding the hidden moisture leak causing the mold.
2. **Containment:** Sealing off the affected area to prevent cross-contamination.
3. **HEPA Air Filtration:** Scrubbing the air to remove invisible spores.
4. **Physical Removal & Antimicrobial Treatment:** Killing roots deep within surfaces.
5. **Post-Remediation Verification:** Ensuring the air quality has returned to healthy levels.

## Factors That Influence Your Final Quote

If you receive a quote from **MoldGuard Kenya**, here is exactly what drives the cost:

- **Size of the Infestation:** The larger the square footage covered by mold, the more labor, equipment, and antimicrobial products are required.
- **Location & Accessibility:** Mold growing in easy-to-reach places (like a bare wall) is cheaper to remove than mold growing inside an attic or crawlspace.
- **Type of Material Infected:** Non-porous surfaces (glass, tiles) can be cleaned and treated. Porous materials (drywall, insulation, carpets) usually must be removed and disposed of safely.
- **Type of Mold:** Highly toxic strains, such as *Stachybotrys chartarum* (Black Mold), require much stricter containment protocols and advanced protective gear for our technicians.

## The Hidden Costs of DIY Mold Removal

Many homeowners attempt to save money by purchasing bleach and scrubbing the mold themselves. **This is a dangerous and expensive mistake.**

1. **Bleach Doesn't Kill Mold Roots:** Bleach contains 90% water. When applied to porous surfaces, the chlorine evaporates while the water travels deep into the material, actually *feeding* the surviving mold roots. The mold will return within weeks, often much worse.
2. **Spreading the Infestation:** Scrubbing dry mold launches millions of microscopic spores into the air. What was a localized bathroom problem can quickly become a whole-house infestation through your HVAC system.
3. **Medical Bills:** Exposure to agitated toxic mold spores can cause severe respiratory distress, asthma attacks, and mold toxicity, leading to costly medical bills.
