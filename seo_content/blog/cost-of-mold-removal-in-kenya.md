# Understanding the Cost of Mold Removal in Kenya

When property owners discover mold, their first question is almost always: *"How much will this cost to fix?"* It is a valid concern. However, in the mold remediation industry, there is no one-size-fits-all price tag. The cost of professional mold removal in Kenya varies significantly based on several crucial variables. 

At **MoldGuard Kenya**, we believe in absolute transparency. In this guide, we break down exactly what factors influence the cost of professional mold remediation and why attempting to take the cheap DIY route often ends up costing far more in the long run.

## Factors That Influence the Cost of Remediation

### 1. The Size of the Infestation
The most prominent factor driving costs is the square footage of the contaminated area. 
- A small, localized patch of mold under a kitchen sink will cost significantly less to remediate than a widespread infestation covering an entire basement or extending through the HVAC ductwork.
- **Tip:** This is why early detection is critical. The sooner you call professionals, the smaller the area they have to treat, saving you money.

### 2. The Root Cause of the Moisture
Mold removal is useless if you do not fix the water source. The cost of the remediation project often depends heavily on how the water is entering the home:
- Drying out a bathroom that simply lacks a ventilation fan is relatively inexpensive.
- Repairing a damaged roof, replacing severely leaking internal plumbing, or excavating a foundation to fix rising damp will add significantly to the overall project cost.

### 3. The Type of Surface Material Compressed
Mold grows differently on different materials.
- **Non-Porous Surfaces:** Mold on glass, metal, or solid bathroom tiles can usually be cleaned and sanitized without needing to destroy the material. This is a cheaper process.
- **Porous Materials:** If mold has deeply infiltrated drywall (gypsum), wooden support beams, ceiling boards, or carpeting, these materials cannot simply be "cleaned." They must be safely demolished, removed, safely disposed of as biohazardous waste, and physically replaced. The reconstruction aspect heavily influences the final bill.

### 4. Required Equipment and Containment
True professional remediation requires massive amounts of specialized gear.
- To prevent toxic spores from spreading to your bedrooms while we clean the living room, we must construct temporary physical containment walls using heavy-duty plastic sheeting and run industrial **HEPA Air Scrubbers** to create negative air pressure.
- We also deploy commercial dehumidifiers to execute structural drying. The longer these heavily powered machines need to run, the higher the operational cost.

## The Hidden Cost of "Cheap" DIY Solutions
Many property owners try to save money by having their domestic staff scrub the walls with bleach, or by hiring a regular cleaning service. **This is highly dangerous and financially short-sighted.**

1. **Medical Costs:** Agitating mold without hazmat suits and HEPA filtration exposes you directly to massive spore counts and toxic mycotoxins. The resulting respiratory infections and severe allergic reactions often result in costly hospital visits.
2. **Property Contamination:** If a room is not sealed properly during DIY cleaning, you are blowing spores throughout the rest of your house. A problem that was localized to a bathroom will suddenly spread to your bedrooms and clothing.
3. **The Mold Returns:** Bleach does not kill mold roots in porous materials. You will spend money fixing the paint and scrubbing the walls, only for the exact same mold to return a month later. You end up paying for the "cheap" fix multiple times before finally calling a professional.
