Complete Application Guide for Maximum Effectiveness

Preparation Phase:

Before application, ensure adequate ventilation by opening windows and doors. Good airflow optimizes drying time and effectiveness. Remove loose debris, dust, and surface dirt from affected areas—this allows the formula to penetrate more effectively into mold-infested materials.

Step-by-Step Application:

Step 1 – Shake Thoroughly: Shake the MoldKill Max bottle vigorously for 30 seconds to ensure proper mixing of active ingredients. This step is crucial for optimal antimicrobial performance.

Step 2 – Spray Generously: Hold the bottle 6-8 inches (15-20cm) from the surface and spray until the area is thoroughly saturated. Don’t worry about over-application—generous coverage ensures better penetration and complete protection. Target visible mold, surrounding areas, and potential problem zones.

Step 3 – Allow Contact Time: Let MoldKill Max sit on the surface for 10-15 minutes without wiping or disturbing. This contact time is essential for the formula to penetrate deeply and eliminate hidden mold structures beneath the surface.

Step 4 – Agitate for Heavy Mold: For heavily molded areas with thick mold growth, gently scrub with a soft-bristled brush after the contact time. This agitation helps break up stubborn mold colonies and improves formula penetration into infected materials.

Step 5 – Final Protective Application: Apply a second coat of MoldKill Max and allow it to air dry completely. This is critical: do not wipe or rinse the treated surface. The dried residue forms the protective antimicrobial barrier that prevents regrowth for up to 6 months.

Post-Treatment Care:

Monitor treated areas over the first week to ensure complete mold elimination. Most mold dies within 24-48 hours, but some deeply embedded colonies may take up to a week to completely disappear. Avoid using other cleaning products on treated areas for at least one week to allow the protective barrier to fully establish and cure.

Common Mold Problem Areas in Kenyan Homes

Bathrooms: The number one mold hotspot due to steam from hot showers meeting cooler surfaces. Focus treatment on shower tiles, grout lines, ceiling corners, behind toilets, under sinks, around bathtub edges, shower curtains, and bathroom walls.

Kitchens: Cooking steam and food preparation moisture create ideal mold conditions. Treat areas around sinks, behind refrigerators, under cabinets, around dishwashers, pantry walls, ceiling corners near stoves, and any areas with water exposure.

Bedrooms: Poor ventilation especially during rainy seasons allows mold growth on walls, ceilings, closet interiors, behind furniture, window frames, and corners. Bedroom mold is particularly concerning due to prolonged exposure during sleep.

Storage Rooms: Limited air circulation and potential moisture intrusion make storage areas prime mold locations. Treat walls, floors, shelving, boxes, and any stored items showing mold signs.

Basements & Ground Floors: Below-grade spaces in multi-story buildings face moisture issues from ground contact. Comprehensive treatment of walls, floors, support columns, and ceiling areas prevents extensive mold colonization.

Behind Furniture: Furniture placed against exterior walls creates air pockets where moisture condenses and mold thrives. Regular treatment of wall surfaces behind beds, sofas, wardrobes, and large furniture pieces prevents hidden mold growth.
